Application ProgressEducation, Health and Care Plan

This page provides you with a guide to how your application request for assessment is progressing and what you can expect to happen next.

Click here to see our useful video that explains how the EHCP process works.

1: Request

Click here to view a clip about what happens at this stage and what to expect

This will take roughly 6 weeks from the date that we received the request

The Local Authority will look at all the paperwork received and decide whether or not an assessment is necessary.

You will be notified in writing of the decision


2: Assessing Needs

Click here to view a clip about what happens at this stage and what to expect

*This will take roughly 6 weeks or a total of 12 weeks from the date the LA received the request

You will be contacted by the SEND Practitioner to arrange a face-to-face meeting. This is your contact in the SEN Team.

The LA is currently asking professionals to carry out assessments and submit their reports.

The LA asks for reports from you, the educational setting, educational psychology, social care and health as a statutory requirement.

The LA will also ask any other professionals involved or identified by you at the Face to Face meeting.


3: Drafting Plan

Click here to view a clip about what happens at this stage and what to expect

*This will take roughly 2 weeks from the day that the LA receive all the reports called advice

The LA is currently considering all the advice submitted to decide whether or not an Education, Health and Care Plan is the best option.

You will be notified in writing of the decision.


4: Draft Education, Health and Care Plan (In Progress)

Click here to view a clip about what happens at this stage and what to expect

*This will take roughly 2 weeks from the decision date

You will receive a decision letter and a copy of the draft plan. You have 15 calendar days to respond to the Draft plan letting us know if you want changes or that you agree with the plan.

You will also be asked about your preferred educational setting. If the LA already has this information a consult will be sent to this setting.


5: Finalising Plan

Click here to view a clip about what happens at this stage and what to expect

*The Final Plan should be issued within 20 weeks of the LA receiving the initial request.

An educational placement has now been agreed. The Draft Plan has been agreed.

The SEND Practitioner will now send you a copy of the Final Plan which will include the name of the educational placement in Part I.

You have a right to mediation and appeal to SENDIST if you do not agree with the decisions that have been made.


ALPHA This is a new service. This service is not currently updated in real-time. The information provided is accurate from the time you were last send a notication. You will be sent a new notifcation as soon as your application advances to the next stage.
